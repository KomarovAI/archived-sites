What Qualifies as a Plumbing Emergency?

A plumbing emergency involves issues that have potential to significantly damage your home or threaten your household health and safety. Minor problems are inconvenient but don’t usually warrant an emergency repair — left unaddressed, however, leaks, corrosion, or other problems can eventually escalate into an emergency. Common plumbing emergencies include:

  • Broken or burst pipes
  • Frozen pipes
  • Sewer line backups
  • Clogged drains
  • Broken water heater
  • Gas leaks

Our Comprehensive Emergency Plumbing Services

At Medley, our plumbing team provides a range of emergency plumbing services designed to address and resolve urgent problems. Whether you’re dealing with a burst pipe or clogged sink drain, our team of skilled plumbers is ready to assist.

Burst pipe repairs

Burst pipes typically result from freezing temperatures, high water pressure, aging pipes, and other circumstances. Sudden water pressure loss and unexplained puddles may signal a burst pipe, potentially one obscured behind your walls. Without urgent attention, flooding from burst pipes causes water damage, sagging ceilings, peeling or bubbling paint, ruined subfloors, mold, and even structural damage.

Our team will arrive with professional tools and equipment for burst pipe repair. We’ll walk you through the repair process and suggest next steps to mitigate damage and safety hazards.

Clogged drains and sewer backups

Slow drains can suddenly become full blockages, sometimes causing one or more backups throughout your home. Medley specializes in fast and long-term fixes for clogged drains, such as:

  • Clogged sink drain repair
  • Sewer line restoration
  • Tree root removal
  • Drain cleaning
  • Augering
  • Hydro-jetting

Water heater emergencies

A malfunctioning water heater can cause major disruptions to your household routine and may even lead to flooding or fire hazards. Corrosion, rust, loose connections, sediment buildup, ignition or power system faults, and other problems may result in unexpected breakdowns and often require immediate attention to avoid further damage or inconvenience.

Leaking or overflowing toilets

Whether from a clog deep in your system or a problem with the components, a leaking or overflowing toilet creates unsanitary messes, flooding and water damage, and wasted water. If plunging your toilet isn’t enough, switch off the water and contact an emergency plumber for overflowing toilet repairs.

Gas leak detection and repairs

Gas leaks are dangerous and require immediate professional attention. Symptoms of a gas leak may include a rotten egg or sulfuric smell, hissing noises, dying plants, and high gas bills. Gas may also result in physical symptoms, such as headaches, nausea, breathing problems, and more. If you suspect a gas leak, evacuate your household and contact a gas leak emergency plumber.

Signs You Need Emergency Plumbing Services

Knowing when to call a local plumber for emergency repair can prevent minor problems from quickly escalating and causing severe and costly damage.

Warning signs

Here are the warning signs that you need emergency plumbing services:

  • Water stains: Discolored spots on your walls or ceilings suggest leaks.
  • Loss of water pressure: A sudden drop in water pressure may indicate a severe leak or burst pipe. This symptom is even more urgent if it affects multiple fixtures.
  • Puddles and pooling water:  Water pooling around your sink, toilet, water heater, or other appliances and fixtures is a clear sign of underlying plumbing issues.
  • Sewage smells: Unpleasant odors from your drains or faucets suggest bacterial overgrowth or blockages in your plumbing system.
  • Gurgling sounds: Gurgling sounds from your toilet, faucets, or drains point to trapped air from a blockage in the system. This can cause pressure buildup that may cause burst pipes or backups.
  • Rotten egg smells: Manufacturers add chemicals to otherwise odorless natural gas that create smells similar to sulfur or rotten eggs so you can smell when you have a natural gas leak.

Why timing matters

For plumbing emergencies, timing is often the difference between simple repairs and major damage requiring costly restorations. Leaks and clogs may initially seem minor, but left unresolved, they lead to water damage, mold, and structural damage. Slow-draining sinks can lead to complete clogs or backflows, triggering a chain of escalating issues with your plumbing system.

FAQs About Piping and Repiping Services

Can you repair leaking or corroded pipes, or do they need to be replaced?

It depends on the damage to the pipe, but in many cases, it’s better to replace a damaged pipe than try to patch it up. 

What causes pipes to burst, and how can I prevent it?

Common causes of burst pipes include increased water pressure due to clogs, temperature changes cracking the pipe, or thin areas of the pipe giving way due to corrosion. Prevent these issues by scheduling ongoing preventative maintenance with a local plumber near you. Avoid putting hair, oils, and food down the drain, and skip harsh cleaning products that worsen corrosion.

What should I do if I notice a sudden drop in water pressure?

Check for obvious clogs, determine whether the problem is localized to one area or the entire house, and then call a plumber. Your water pressure may have dropped for multiple reasons, but if it’s a burst pipe, you’ll want to take care of it right away.

Why is my water discolored or smelling bad?

Discolored or smelly water may occur due to spring runoff, but if it’s a new issue, it could be caused by corroding copper pipes or materials entering a crack in your plumbing. A professional plumber can help you find the root of the issue and fix it. 

What causes noisy pipes, and how can they be fixed?

You may hear banging, humming, or vibration sounds if you have high water pressure, air in the pipes, or clogged pipes. A Medley plumber will identify the sound and its origin and fix the issue for you.

What Is a Tankless Water Heater?

A tankless water heater, also known as an on-demand water heater, heats water as needed. Unlike traditional water heaters, which store and heat a large amount of water in a tank, tankless water heaters don’t have a storage tank. Frequently Asked Questions About Drain and Rooter Services

What causes multiple drains in my home to clog at the same time?

Intrusive tree roots, hair, food, non-biodegradable foreign objects, or grease buildup can cause multiple drains in your home to clog at the same time. A broken pipe or mineral buildup may also cause several clogs to occur at once.

What is the difference between drain cleaning and Rooter services?

Drain cleaning typically involves removing a clog from a single drain using a snake or other plumbing tools. Rooter services utilize more complex techniques like hydrojetting, tree root removal, or trenchless sewer repair.

How do trenchless sewer repair options work for damaged pipes?

Trenchless sewer repair fixes cracked or collapsed pipes without having to dig the earth around them or your home’s foundation to access them. This technology may use a special liner or epoxy coating to repair the pipes from the inside, or it may require bursting a pipe while simultaneously replacing the broken section with a new one remotely.
